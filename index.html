Multiverse theory

Introduction to the Multiverse theory.

Though popularized by fictional works like Marvel and DC the idea of multiple universes has been around for centuries, and realistically the idea isn't actually that far fetched. With technological advances happening at insane rates, the theory becomes stronger by the day.

Parental universes

The multiverse theory is split between theory crafters. That said, the one i personally find most likely is the idea of parent universes. This theory is based on the fact that we already have old universes which existed long before our own universe came to be. This theory can be backed up by other theories and hypothesis too, suggesting either technological advances from previous universes or random situasjons in that universe which creates a new one.

The big bang theory.

To make sense out of the multiverse theory, we need to look at two other universally accepted theories which holds a strong scientific backing and evidentiality, starting with the Big Bang. The Big Bang is said to be the start of our universe. No matter which variant of the big bang you're viewing, that part is still the truth if you follow any scientific views of the Big Bang. Every single version of the Big Bang, has everything condensed in to the beginning. This gives our universe a starting point which then later "exploded" in a sence, which made the universe expand and become what our universe is today. As to why this is important for the multiverse theory, it basically shows that our universe has a simular beginning to animal births. More spesifically, the chicken and the egg. The universe also has a starting point which begs the question, what created the starting point? A possible answer to that is an event simular to black/white hole theories or something compleatly different. Still it carries a basis of multiple universes and a "Parent universe".
